Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about the creative process, design philosophy, and approach behind architectural photography and visualization.

My inspiration often comes from geometry, light, and the rhythm of structures. I enjoy capturing how architecture interacts with its environment—whether it’s the sharp angles of modern design or the organic flow of historical buildings.

I focus on projects that tell a story—spaces that reflect innovation, purpose, and harmony. Whether it’s a minimal modern structure or a restored heritage building, I look for design that balances creativity with function.

I use a full-frame DSLR with a tilt-shift lens to correct perspective distortion, along with a sturdy tripod and natural lighting whenever possible. Post-processing tools like Lightroom and Photoshop help refine tones and symmetry.

Yes, collaboration is key. I often work closely with architects and interior designers to understand the vision behind their projects, ensuring each photograph highlights the intended mood, scale, and spatial relationship.

Clients can contact me directly via the contact form on my website or by email. I typically schedule a consultation to discuss project details, preferred lighting conditions, and desired outcomes before the shoot.
